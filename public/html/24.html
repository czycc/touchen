Special effects production

In film and television, artificial creation of illusion and illusion is called special effects (also known as special effects).Filmmakers use them to avoid putting actors in a dangerous situation, to reduce the cost of making movies, or simply to use them to make movies more compelling.

Miniature model:

Models of people, monsters, buildings and vehicles are used to make special effects.When the model must be moved, we can move them in either of three ways: by hand, by machine, or by electricity.It takes a long time to move them by hand, because the model requires at least 24 actions on the screen for a second.

As one of the indispensable elements in the film industry, special effects have made great contributions to the development of films. Why special effects exist in film and television works?

The content of film and television works and the life/scene in the film are completely fictitious, and there are no real ones, such as monsters, and certain planets.Since it doesn't exist, it needs to be present in film and television, so the professionals in this field need to create and solve it.

Reality can exist, but it's impossible to make a particular effect, and it also requires special effects,For example, if someone jumps from the 30th floor, it's impossible for an actor to do that, which requires a computer to synthesize.In addition, the reality can be completely presented, but because the cost is too high or the effect is bad, special effects must be used to solve it. Such as the plane explosion in the war movies.
